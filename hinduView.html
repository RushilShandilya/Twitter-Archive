Hindu View of Christianity and Islam

Hindus don’t realize but their own religion, culture, society & civilization is interpreted by others. And not just indifferent others. The story of Hindu dharma is being told by its worst enemies: the monotheists.

2. They are enemies because they very clearly and actively seek the destruction of Hindu dharma, Hindu society and the Hindu way of life. In the Christian and Islamic scheme of things, Hindus are the worst sort of beings because they are ‘idolaters’.

3. It is natural for civilizations to have enemies. What is not natural is that Hindus turn against their own benign culture and civilization. That they start seeing Hindu dharma from the Judeo- Christian lens. But this is exactly what has happened.

4. Our media, academia, politics and even society has been infested with interpretations of our own dharma from the eyes of our worst enemies. This game has become so advanced that we are not even aware that we are looking at ourselves from the enemy’s eyes.

5. The saints of Hindu Renaissance like Swami Vivekananda and Shri Aurobindo had called for inverting the gaze and most particularly Swami Dayananda had done a pūrva pakṣa of Islam and Christinaity but that movement got aborted during the run for independence.

6. It was Shri Ram Swarup, who for the first time after this, gave the call for inverting the gaze. In a way, he along with Shri Sita Ram Goel, inverted the gaze, which meant: looking at Islam, Christianity and Judaism from a Hindu point of view.

7. It was an attempt to actually tell the Hindu story from a native point of view and to reclaim our fundamental right to look at the world in our own ways; to reclaim our ways of knowing, looking and expressing. Shri Ram Swarup did this vocally and deeply through his works.

8. His first observation about Christianity and Islam was that they lacked inner spirituality. Hindu dharma recommends meditation to find the ultimate truth, these religions said that the ultimate truth is found and written in their Book. Shri Ram Swarup says:

9. "What forcibly strikes a discriminating student of the Bible is that its god lacks interiority. Though the Bible exhorts its followers to love their god with all their heart, yet throughout its long career there is nothing to show that it knows of a "god or gods in the heart".

10. Shri Ram Swarup says that the Judeo-Christian and Islamic God also lacks universality despite all claims to the contrary. This God seems to be concerned overwhelmingly with the affairs of Middle Eastern deserts and was extremely partisan to one group of people.

11. It is okay to respect one’s deity but if one starts thinking that other gods are evil and should be eliminated, the trouble starts. The Biblical God is like that. Shri Ram Swarup says that this disparaging thoughts about others’ gods comes from rājasika-tāmasika citta bhūmīs.

12. The most defining feature of Prophetic Monotheism – One True God – was established by Judaism itself. "By the time Jesus came the cult of a one god and a special people was well established." And he added the Seal of Prophecy to it, saying that no more Prophets can come.

13. The lethal combination of Prophetic Monotheism was building. Shri Ram Swarup has dwelt upon this process and nature of monotheism in his works and most importantly he has analyzed that process through the point of view of yogic spirituality.
